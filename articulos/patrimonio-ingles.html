Heritage Day was born in Uruguay in 1995, driven by architect José Luis Livni as president of the National Commission for Historical, Artistic and Cultural Heritage, with the aim of promoting love for the cultural legacies that shape Uruguayan identity. Inspired by France's Journées du Patrimoine, it grew year after year, drawing over 500,000 participants by 2000. What began as a single day focused on Montevideo's historic buildings expanded to two days and spread nationwide, also embracing music, dances and popular traditions. Each edition centers on a specific theme; in 2025 it commemorated the Bicentennial of Independence, and in 2026 it reaches its 32nd edition.
